A Reflection on my Blog Experience

Today will be a short discussion on my experience so far on my blog and where it will be going in the future.

It's been 9 weeks and phase 0 is coming to an end seemingly as quickly as it started. To some degree there is a point there; it has only been 9 weeks. That being said it's incredible to think about how far I've come in just 9 weeks.

As I mentioned in my About Me I do have some limited experience with coding. I took some Visual Basic and Java at community college as well as C++ when I was pursuing my B.S. in Mechanical Engineering. I've had experience with and exposure to the logic of programming. I understood flow control and iterations before I started DBC. When I was learning Java it was as a tool to the a larger concept of object oriented programming, so I knew about using classes and constructors and scope of variables, all those concepts before I even started in DBC.

All that being said, I have never used a markdown language before. I never knew anything about HTML and CSS. I've known how to access page info for a long time, but it's always been one of those things where I hit a wrong button and all this muddled nonsense popped up, and I had to figure out how to make it go away. One of the most rewarding things for me was being able to look back on that info and being able to sort of read and understand a little of what was going on. That was only after the first week of html. Now I didn't really understand how the styling worked, or I didn't really have any understanding of how the JavaScript worked or even what it was doing, really.

There is also something else. It's the integration of it all. How does it all fit together, and how does what you do in one language affect what happens in the other language. We have gotten to that a little bit. We saw how to integrate CSS into an HTML document, and we've played around with different styling attributes to see how it affects the html and how it's displayed. Just this week we have gotten a slight glimpse into how JavaScript can be paired with HTML as well to create and add features to a web page.

We have also been blogging about topics we are learning as we learn them. This helps to solidify our understanding of these topics as well as broaden that understanding by going out and searching beyond what the challenges provide.

It's something that has been a bit tedious at times for me, but a necessatu evil. I understand the importance and significance of expanding on a topic. I know it has helped me to further understand a topic to go out and do additional research. I have always been more of a hands on learner, however. It was the same way in college. I got more enjoyment out of the learning process if I was seeing exactly how the proncocples and concepts apploed in real life. So too with progreamming. I understand the benefits to searching and expanding on a topic. I would have rather had, however, a few extra assigned challenges to explore the topic further. That's just how I learn best and it's differenct for everyone.

As far as where I want this blog to go, I am unsure. There is an implicit expectation that we will continue to blog into phase 1 and beyond. What I will be blogging about, however, is anything but clear. Perhaps it will be a blog about my experience in the next phases to try and shed some light on what to expect for others coming into this unknown experience as I am now. To that end perhaps I should do the same afterwards to show what life is like after DBC. Who knows?

The point of these blogs has been to educate ourselves as well as others, so hopefully I can continue that trend moving forward. I am not sure what awaits me in week 10 or what it will be like afterwards. It's going to be a whole new experience and a world entirely different from the one I come from. The possibilities are nearly endless, so let's go exploring.
